Why Watermark Your Images?

Watermarking serves multiple important purposes:

  • Copyright protection: Clearly identify images as your intellectual property, making it harder for others to use them without permission.
  • Brand recognition: Add your logo or name to build brand awareness when images are shared across social media or websites.
  • Professional presentation: Subtle watermarks can give your portfolio or gallery a more professional appearance.
  • Deterrent effect: Visible watermarks discourage unauthorized downloading and sharing of your work.

Best Practices for Watermarking

To get the most effective results from your watermarks:

  • Position strategically: Place watermarks where they're visible but don't distract from the main subject. Corners are popular choices, though center placement offers maximum protection.
  • Adjust opacity carefully: Balance visibility with subtlety. Too transparent and the watermark is easily removed; too opaque and it detracts from your image quality.
  • Use appropriate sizing: Text should be readable but not overwhelming. Image watermarks are automatically scaled to reasonable proportions.
  • Match your brand: Choose colors and fonts that align with your brand identity for consistent professional presentation.
  • Consider your output format: PNG preserves transparency best, while JPEG offers smaller file sizes. WebP provides a modern balance of quality and compression.
