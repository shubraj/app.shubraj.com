Inspect Your HTTP Request Headers

Headers describe how your browser talks to servers—accepted formats, languages, compression, and more. Seeing them helps troubleshoot caching, redirects, and content negotiation.

What to Look For

  • User‑Agent: Identify browser and platform when debugging compatibility.
  • Accept/Encoding: Confirm gzip/brotli and content types supported.
  • Authorization/Cookies: Ensure sensitive headers are sent only when intended.

Notes

  • Proxies and extensions may alter headers. Test in a clean profile when isolating issues.
  • Be careful when sharing headers—redact tokens or session IDs.
