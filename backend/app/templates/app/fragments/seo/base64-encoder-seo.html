Encode Text to Base64 for Safe Transport and Storage

Base64 encoding turns arbitrary bytes into plain text made of A–Z, a–z, 0–9, + and /. Use it when you need a text‑only representation that won’t break in JSON, cookies, environment variables, or simple logs.

Where Base64 Helps

  • Embedding small assets: Inline icons or fonts in CSS or HTML for quick demos.
  • Text‑only channels: Move binary data through systems that expect UTF‑8 text.
  • Diagnostics: Compare encoded payloads across services for troubleshooting.

Good Practices

  • Keep large binaries out of Base64 in production—file storage or CDNs are better.
  • Remember that Base64 grows data by roughly 33%.
  • Use URL‑safe variants when putting data in URLs or path segments.
