Understanding DPI and Image Quality

DPI (Dots Per Inch) determines the resolution of converted images:

  • 72 DPI: Suitable for web display and screen viewing. Smaller file sizes, faster loading.
  • 200 DPI: Recommended for most use cases. Good balance between quality and file size, suitable for printing small documents.
  • 300 DPI: High quality for printing. Produces larger files but ensures crisp text and graphics when printed.

Higher DPI values create larger files but maintain better quality when zoomed or printed. For screen viewing and web use, 200 DPI provides an excellent balance. For professional printing, 300 DPI ensures publication-quality results.

PNG vs JPEG Format

Choosing the right format depends on your needs:

  • PNG Format: Lossless compression preserves perfect quality, supports transparency, ideal for documents with text, graphics, or when you need exact pixel-perfect reproduction. Files are typically larger but maintain quality.
  • JPEG Format: Lossy compression creates smaller files, suitable for photos and complex images. Better for reducing file sizes when perfect quality isn't critical. JPEG doesn't support transparency.

For text-heavy PDFs or documents with sharp graphics, PNG is recommended. For photo-heavy PDFs or when file size is a concern, JPEG may be more appropriate.
