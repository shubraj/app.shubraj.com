Hash Passwords with bcrypt for Safer Storage

bcrypt is a password hashing function designed to be slow and resistant to brute‑force attacks. Use it to derive a hash from a password before storing credentials in a database.

What Matters with bcrypt

  • Cost factor: Higher cost increases CPU work; choose a value your servers can handle.
  • Unique salts: bcrypt generates a salt per hash to protect against rainbow tables.
  • No plain text: Never store or log raw passwords—only the bcrypt hash.

Operational Tips

  • Benchmark cost values on your actual infrastructure.
  • Rotate credentials and enforce strong password policies.
  • Pair hashing with rate limiting and MFA for real defenses.
